The best engineers I've worked with aren't the ones who write the cleanest code. They're the ones who listen.

I used to think being good at this meant knowing more — more languages, more patterns, more frameworks. And yeah, that stuff matters. But the moments that actually changed how I work were conversations, not commits.

A mentor who told me to slow down. A teammate who pushed back on my idea and was right. A user who described their frustration in a way that made me rethink an entire feature.

Code is a means. People are the point.

I'm still learning this. Probably always will be.
