Most of what I build starts with a feeling, not a brief. A URL shortener because the ones out there felt bloated. A media converter because I got tired of sketchy websites. A productivity timer because I wanted something that respected silence.

Nobody asked for any of it. That's the point.

There's a difference between building to solve someone else's problem and building to scratch your own itch. Both are valid. But the second one teaches you something the first one can't — what you actually care about when nobody's watching.

The best things I've made came from that space. No stakeholders, no sprint planning, no user stories. Just a question: *what if this existed?*

I think more people should build things nobody asked for. Not to ship. Not to monetize. Just to see what happens when you follow curiosity without a deadline.
